Functions of Digestive System

Our digestive system have two major functions which is digestion and absorption. Digestion is the breakdown of food into small molecules which then be absorbed by our body. The whole function of digestive system includes: Ingestion, Mastication, Propulsion, Secretion, Digestion, Absorption and Elimination.

Ingestion

It is also called as Feeding. It is simply the intake of food into the body.

Mastication

It is the process by which food taken into the mouth is chewed by the teeth.

Propulsion

It is the movement of food from one end of the digestive tract to the other.

Secretion

As food moves through the digestive tract, secretions are added to lubricate, liquefy, and digest the food.

Digestion

It is the breakdown of large organic molecules into their component parts.

Absorption

It is the process whereby digested food materials are taken into the body cells and are converted into another substance such as protein or used to provide energy.

Elimination

It is the process by which the waste products of digestion are removed from the body.
